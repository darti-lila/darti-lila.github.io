SeaOrb

Serial controlled submarine

Project Description

SEAORB (Your Eye Underwater) is a DIY remotely operated underwater vehicle (ROV) designed to explore Albanian marine fauna using GPS navigation, real-time camera streaming, and AI-powered species identification, making marine biology research accessible and non-invasive.

SEAORB is a modular underwater exploration platform capable of diving, navigating, and documenting marine life through remote control. The submarine realizes this by using a ballast system with water pumps to control depth (filling 1L chambers in 40 seconds for submersion), four DC motors with counter-rotating propellers for 3D mobility, and a 1MP camera with dual LED systems (ambient and focus lights) for underwater visibility. The beauty of SEAORB is that it's completely open-source and built from accessible materials—PLA and PP plastic tubes printed on standard 3D printers, off-the-shelf electronics controlled by Arduino Nano, and a web interface for real-time control via USB-Ethernet connection up to 10 meters. SEAORB has a modular chamber design with separate compartments for power (dual 12V lithium batteries), water ballast, and electronics, allowing for easy maintenance and future upgrades like AI fauna recognition systems.

Key Features:

  • Variable depth ballast system using water pumps (12.5ml/s flow rate per pump)
  • Four-motor propulsion with 5-blade propellers at 500 RPM for precise 3D navigation
  • Web-based control interface with keyboard controls for motors, pumps, and lights
  • Arduino Nano microcontroller with L298N motor drivers and relay-controlled circuits
  • Real-time camera streaming (1MP resolution) via USB-Ethernet hybrid connection

This was my high school senior project at Harry Fultz Institute in Albania (2022-2023 academic year) completed with Albion Spaho and Erlis Ciko. The biggest challenges were waterproofing the electronics (we used rigid polyurethane insulation and silicon sealing) and balancing the submarine's buoyancy—getting the center of gravity right required multiple iterations of the chamber layout. My role focused on designing and building all the electronic circuits on perfboards, implementing power regulation with LM7809 and LM7812 voltage regulators, and extensive testing to minimize electrical noise. We were inspired by DIY Perks' submersible and PeterSripol's magnet fishing ROV, but SEAORB improves on both with true variable ballast (not preset weights) and full modularity.

NOTE: This is a proof-of-concept prototype tested only in controlled dry environments—full waterproofing and underwater testing would require industrial-grade sealing methods and pressure testing. The submarine uses hydrodynamic design principles validated in AirShaper CFD simulations, and follows ISO 19673-19678 standards for remotely controlled watercraft. Future improvements include wireless communication (currently tethered), better waterproofing with marine-grade enclosures, GPS positioning for surface navigation, and implementing the planned AI species identification system using computer vision. The project demonstrates that sophisticated marine research tools can be built affordably with open-source designs, making underwater exploration accessible to students and researchers in developing countries!
